# G-PRO MASTER HEALTH PROTOCOL

> **Purpose:** Translates genetic risks into actionable protocols. Organized by 3 primary axes (alcohol, blood sugar, detox) plus secondary domains.

---

## AXIS 1: ALCOHOL — HIGHEST RISK

**Genetic evidence:** ALDH2 rs671 (Glu504Lys) — Asian Flush variant. Acetaldehyde accumulates → carcinogenic. [HIGH confidence]

**Compounding factors:**
- Emotional eating/stress-drinking tendency (DDR1, TCF4) → may seek alcohol when stressed
- F&B industry → constant exposure
- Poor detox capacity (CYP1A1, CYP3A bottom 10%) → additional toxin burden

**Protocol:**
1. **Zero alcohol is the only safe target.** Even small amounts increase esophageal cancer risk ×6-8.
2. **Alternative for social situations:** Non-alcoholic beer/spritzers. Prepare a line: "I'm allergic — my face turns red" (genetically true).
3. **Stress management replacement:** When urge to drink hits → walk 10 min or call someone. The genetic tendency to seek alcohol under stress is real.
4. **Work (L'Usine):** As Head of Operations, you don't need to drink to do business. Lead by example. Stock quality non-alcoholic options.

## AXIS 2: BLOOD SUGAR — HIGH RISK

**Genetic evidence:** IGF2BP2 (TG), SREBF1 (AG) — impaired pancreatic beta cell function. Diabetes risk top 25%. [HIGH confidence]

**Compounding factors:**
- TOFI phenotype (thin outside, fat inside) — BMI 21.5 masks metabolic risk
- Poor carbohydrate metabolism (AMY1-AMY2, ADIPOQ)
- Belly fat tendency (HOXC10 — cortisol-driven storage)
- Low cardiovascular fitness (EPAS1 — poor VO2max)

**Protocol:**
1. **Reduce refined carbs:** White rice → brown rice/cauliflower rice. Bread → limit. Noodles → limit. Target <130g total carbs/day.
2. **Post-meal walk:** 10-15 min after each meal — this single habit has stronger evidence than most diabetes drugs.
3. **Morning cortisol management:** Don't skip breakfast (IF window 12-20 is fine, but break fast with protein+fat, not carbs).
4. **Annual screening:** Fasting glucose + HbA1c every 6 months. Start now (age 42, no recent bloodwork documented).
5. **Weight vigilance:** Despite low BMI, watch for waist circumference increase. Current 63kg at 171cm is fine, but if waist >90cm → intervene immediately.

## AXIS 3: DETOX — VERY POOR

**Genetic evidence:** CYP1A1 (TC), CYP3A (TT) — bottom 10% for toxin clearance. [HIGH confidence]

**Protocol:**
1. **Absolute no-smoke zone:** Avoid secondhand smoke completely. This includes restaurant/bar environments where smoke lingers.
2. **Limit alcohol** (see Axis 1) — your liver cannot handle the double burden.
3. **Air quality:** Consider an air purifier if living in high-traffic area.
4. **Medication caution:** You metabolize drugs differently. Always mention "I have slow detox genetics" to doctors when prescribed new medications.
5. **Diet support:** Cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, cabbage) support phase 2 detox — eat daily.

## SECONDARY: NUTRITION

### Fasting Protocol (Current 16:8)
- **Verdict:** Compatible with genetics
- **Adjustment:** Break fast with protein+fat, not carbs. Your carb metabolism is poor — a carb-heavy first meal spikes glucose.
- **Hydration:** Your cognition genes (AFF3, CAMKV) require good hydration — drink water during fasting window too.

## SECONDARY: INJURY PREVENTION

**Genetic evidence:** IGF2 (CC), MMP3 (TC) — injury risk top 8%. [HIGH confidence]

**Compounding factors:** Low muscle strength (TGFA), poor cardiovascular fitness (EPAS1)

**Protocol:**
1. **No high-impact sports.** No crossfit, no intense running, no contact sports.
2. **Acceptable exercise:** Swimming, cycling (low resistance), brisk walking, yoga, pilates, light resistance training.
3. **Footwear:** Quality orthopedic insoles mandatory.
4. **Warm-up:** Minimum 10 min dynamic warm-up before any exercise.
5. **Avoid quinolone antibiotics** (e.g., Ciprofloxacin, Levofloxacin) — increased tendon rupture risk with your MMP3 genotype.
6. **Listen to knee/ankle signals:** Your MMP3 variant means connective tissue heals slower. Any joint pain → rest, don't push through.
