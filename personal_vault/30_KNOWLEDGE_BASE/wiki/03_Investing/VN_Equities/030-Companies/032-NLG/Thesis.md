## 5. VALUATION

**Assumptions:** ~485M shares outstanding. Market price confirmed: VND 26,000.

**TTM Metrics:**
- TTM parent PAT (Apr 2025 - Mar 2026): 701B (FY2025) - 108B (Q1 2025) + 68B (Q1 2026) = **661B**
- TTM EPS: **1,363 VND/share**
- BVPS (Dec 2025): **26,100 VND/share** (parent equity ~12,662B / 485M shares)
- Normalized 2yr avg PAT: (518B + 701B) / 2 = **610B**

### Spec 5 Methods

| Method | Calculation | Fair Value/Share | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Munger Owner Earnings (15x)** | (661 TTM PAT + 52 DA - 10 Capex) x 15 / 485M | **21,742** | MOD |
| **Lynch PEGY = 1.2** | EPS 1,363 x 1.2 x (5% G + 3.85% Y) = 1,363 x 10.62 | **14,475** | LOW |
| **Damodaran SOTP** | Book adj: land bank 1.3x (8,381B to 10,895B) + JVs 1.15x (5,353B to 6,156B) -> adjusted parent equity ~16,047B / 485M | **33,100** | LOW |
| **P/E Peer (18x)** | TTM EPS 1,363 x median peer P/E (DXG ~20x, NVL ~15x, PDR ~18x) | **24,534** | MOD |
| **Damodaran 5yr DCF (WACC 10%, g 3%)** | FCFF 652B (2yr avg PAT 610 + DA 52 - Capex 10), grows 3%, TV 2%, PV = ~8,670B / 485M | **17,900** | LOW |

### Composite

| Range | Composite | vs Current (26,000) |
|---|---|---|
| 5 methods equal weight | **22,350** | +16% overvalued |
| 5 methods ex-SOTP and ex-DCF (higher confidence) | **23,138** | +12% overvalued |
| Book value only (P/B 1.0x) | **26,100** | At parity |

**Price: VND 26,000 | P/E (TTM): 19.1x | P/B: 1.0x**

### Key Observations

1. The 5 spec-required methods range widely (14,475 to 33,100). The spread reflects the difficulty of valuing a lumpy real estate developer on earnings alone.
2. At 26,000, NLG trades at **exactly book value** (BVPS 26,100). Among VN developers, P/B of 1.0x is at the very low end (KDH ~3x, DXG ~2x, VHM ~1.2x). [MOD]
3. The SOTP method (~33,100) is the most conceptually appropriate for a developer but requires project-level land bank data for confidence. With conservative land market uplift (1.3x book), NLG appears undervalued. [LOW]
4. Lynch PEGY (14,475) systematically undervalues developers Ã¢â‚¬â€ PEGY assumes stable earnings growth which real estate companies do not have. [LOW]
5. The 5yr DCF (17,900) suffers from the same issue: terminal value dominates (69% of PV) since near-term cash flows are small relative to land bank value. DCF is a poor fit for asset-heavy developers. [LOW]

## 7. RISK FACTORS

1. **Revenue concentration risk [HIGH]:** Q1 2026 shows NLG increasingly reliant on project transfers vs organic housing sales. Project transfers are lumpy and buyer-dependent.
2. **Parent PAT volatility [HIGH]:** Parent PAT swung from +35% (FY2025) to -37% (Q1 2026) due to minority attribution shifts. Earnings quality for parent shareholders is inconsistent.
3. **Pipeline replenishment [MOD]:** Inventory halved to 8,677B. Without new land acquisitions, future revenue may be constrained.
4. **Thong tu 99/2025 [UNKNOWN]:** New accounting circular effective Jan 1, 2026. NLG is still evaluating impact. Could affect revenue recognition.
5. **Interest rate sensitivity [LOW]:** Low D/E (0.37x) provides insulation, but interest cost of 86B/quarter still material.
